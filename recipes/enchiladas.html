Enchiladas

Enchiladas are a traditional Mexican dish with origins dating back to the Aztec Empire and earlier Mesoamerican civilizations. Indigenous peoples in Mexico were known to use corn tortillas as a staple food, filling them with various ingredients and rolling or folding them for convenience.

The term "enchilada" comes from the Spanish word enchilar, which means "to season with chili." This reflects the practice of dipping tortillas in chili sauces, a hallmark of the dish.

Ingridients

  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste

For the Filling

  • 2 cups shredded cooked chicken, beef, or beans (for a vegetarian option)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a blend)
  • 1 small onion, diced (optional)

For Assembly

  • 8-10 corn tortillas (or flour tortillas, if preferred)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese for topping
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, chopped cilantro, diced tomatoes, avocado slices, or jalapeños.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Sauce
    • In a medium saucepan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
    • Add the flour and whisk until lightly golden and bubbly (about 1-2 minutes).
    • Stir in the chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, and oregano.
    • Gradually pour in the broth, whisking constantly to avoid lumps.
    • Simmer the sauce for 5-7 minutes until slightly thickened. Add salt to taste and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Filling
    • In a large bowl, mix the shredded chicken (or other filling), 1 cup of shredded cheese, and diced onion if using.

  3. Soften the Tortillas
    • Heat a small amount of oil in a skillet over medium heat.
    • Lightly fry each tortilla for a few seconds on both sides to soften them. This prevents cracking when rolling.

  4. Assemble the Enchiladas
    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    • Spread a thin layer of enchilada sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.
    • Place a tortilla on a flat surface, spoon some filling into the center, and roll it tightly.
    • Place the rolled tortilla seam-side down in the baking dish. Repeat with the remaining tortillas and filling.

  5. Top and Bake
    • Pour the enchilada sauce evenly over the rolled tortillas, ensuring they are fully coated.
    • Sprinkle the remaining shredded cheese on top.
    • Cover the dish with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for another 5-10 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden.

  6. Serve
    • Garnish with your favorite toppings like sour cream, cilantro, or avocado slices.
    • Serve warm and enjoy your homemade enchiladas!
